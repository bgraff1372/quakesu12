Key Considerations for Team Managers

\

Effective tournament selection involves more than just dates and locations. Here are some key factors from the report to keep in mind for a successful season.

\ \
\ Registration Logistics & Deadlines\
\

Timely registration is critical. Application deadlines can be months in advance, and popular tournaments fill up quickly. Always double-check deadlines on the official tournament website.

\
    \
  • Roster Management: Most tournaments use platforms like GotSport and require rosters to be finalized and uploaded before the event. Rosters are often frozen a week prior.
    • \
  • Required Documents: Be prepared with official team rosters, laminated player passes, and signed medical release forms for check-in.
    • \
  • Payment: Credit card payments are common but may include a small processing fee.
    • \
\
\
\ \
\ "Stay to Play" Policies\
\

Larger tournaments (like the MVLA Bay Area Fall Cup) may enforce a "Stay to Play" policy. This means teams traveling over a certain distance (e.g., 80 miles) must book their hotel accommodations through the tournament's designated travel partner. This is a crucial logistical and financial consideration to factor into your team's budget.

\
\
\ \
\ Matching Competitive Level\
\

Choosing the right competitive level is vital for a positive experience. Divisions are designed to create balanced competition.

\
    \
  • High Comp (Premier/Gold): For top-level, competitive club teams.
    • \
  • Mid Comp (Silver/Bronze): For intermediate teams seeking a good challenge.
    • \
  • Low Comp (Copper/Rec/Dev): For developing teams or those focused more on participation and fun.
    • \
\

Always assess your team's current skill level honestly to select the best-fit division.
